Winter sports

Skiing

Switzerland is renowned for its exceptional ski resorts, offering a diverse range of skiing experiences for all levels. From the picturesque villages of the Swiss Alps to the high-altitude ski areas, Switzerland has something for every skier. With over 300 ski resorts, Switzerland boasts some of the most spectacular mountain scenery, state-of-the-art ski lifts, and a wide range of slopes for all abilities.

The Swiss ski resorts are known for their high-quality infrastructure, including modern cable cars, high-speed chair lifts, and efficient ski lifts.

Many resorts also offer a range of amenities, such as mountain restaurants, après-ski bars, and luxurious accommodations.

Ice Hockey

In September 1908, the Swiss Ice Hockey Association became a member of the International Ice Hockey Federation.[5] Bellerive would go on to win the first national championship just one year later. Within a decade, the Swiss Ice Hockey Association expanded to 23 distinguished clubs.

Switzerland has produced several global iconic hockey talents that have made it to the NHL level. While not a traditional hockey powerhouse, Switzerland continues to aggressively enforce a continued & growing presence in the NHL, as well as international competition.

Hiking

Switzerland has a dense and extremely well-marked network of hiking trails. There are routes for everyone: town and city walks, easy valley hikes, and all sorts of mountain trails. Hiking is the best way to feel close to Switzerland’s nature. It’s important to prepare well, even if a hike seems simple.

Explore the best hikes in Switzerland with immaculate lakes, ridges and alpine huts, all woven together into your ultimate Swiss Alps hiking guide. Switzerland is wired for hiking: trains drop you at trailheads, funiculars haul you up to ridges, and half the time there’s a hut on top serving Rösti.
